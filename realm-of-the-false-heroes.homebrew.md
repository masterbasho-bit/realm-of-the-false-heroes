# The Realm of the False Heroes
{{text-align:center
***A Level 9 Interlude for*** ***Tomb of Annihilation***
}}

___

## Synopsis
Weary of the death curse consuming his body and facing a party of adventurers far too powerful for his weakened state, the yuan-ti leader **Ras Nsi** invokes a desperate measure. Utilizing a forbidden artifact gifted to him in a fever dream, he banishes the party to a dimension where their magical might and martial prowess are rendered meaningless: **ExCeL London Comic Con**.

In this bizarre realm of synthetic heroes and cardboard gods, the party is stripped of lethal force. To escape, they must navigate the strange customs of "fandom," survive the judgment of the locals, and win a cosplay tournament against their arch-rivals, **Team Rules Lawyer**. Only by claiming the Grand Prize—the **Amulet of Restoration**—can they shatter the illusion and return to Omu.

## Part 1: The Departure
{{note
**Location:** Area 11, The Throne Room (Fane of the Night Serpent).
}}

**Ras Nsi** looks down upon the party from his hydra-themed throne. His once-imposing form is gaunt, his scales peeling away to reveal rotting flesh beneath. He appears weary, utterly uninterested in a physical fight against foes who radiate such divine and arcane power.

{{descriptive
"You come to me seeking blood? I have little left to spill. You seek to play the hero? Then go... go to the realm where *everyone* plays the hero, where greatness is nothing but a costume and a painted mask!"
}}

With a trembling hand, Ras Nsi produces the **Amulet of False Realities**. The artifact hums with a chaotic, glitching energy, its surface shifting between gold and cheap plastic. He crushes it in his fist.

The sound is not a magical explosion, but a digital screech—like a corrupted audio file. The humidity of the Fane evaporates instantly. The room dissolves, not into darkness, but into a swirl of green mist that smells of snake venom, ozone, and rotting jungle vegetation.

### The "Convention Curse" (Global Rules)
While in this dimension, the physics of Faerûn are rewritten by the mundane laws of Earth, filtered through the lens of a convention. The following rules apply.

{{note
##### 1. Foam Physics
All weapon damage is reduced to **0**. A greataxe hit does not cleave flesh; it bounces harmlessly off the target. However, the force is real enough to knock someone back. A successful hit pushes the target 5 feet and earns **Style Points** (flavor only).
:
##### 2. Magic as SFX
Spells are transmuted into "Special Effects."

* *Fireball* becomes a burst of harmless glitter and dry ice fog.
* *Thunderwave* is a localized bass drop from hidden speakers.
* *Eldritch Blast* looks like a laser pointer or a smoke ring.

These effects deal **no damage**, but they are visually spectacular.
:
##### 3. Class Fantasy Rule (Agency Fix)
The loss of damage does not mean a loss of agency. While spells don't hurt, **they still impose conditions or distractions.**

* A well-timed *Fog Cloud* still blinds enemies.
* *Thaumaturgy* can rattle the environment to intimidate a rival.
* *Hold Person* might manifest as the target getting their costume snagged on scenery.

***DM Ruling:*** If a player uses a spell creatively to distract, blind, or impede a rival, grant **Advantage** to an ally or impose **Disadvantage** on the Rival.
:
##### 4. The Death Curse Anchor
The connection to Chult remains, acting as a grim tether. Healing magic (*Cure Wounds*, *Lay on Hands*, potions) fizzles instantly into stage smoke. The characters **cannot heal** in this dimension. This serves as a constant, nagging reminder that while this world feels safe, the **Death Curse** is still ticking away their lives back home.
}}

## Part 3: The Convention Floor
Before they can enter the tournament, the party must navigate the "locals" and the strange bureaucracy of the convention.

### Encounter A: Peace-Bonding (Fail Forward)
As the party moves through the crowd, a security guard (human, tired eyes, yellow high-vis vest) steps in their path, holding up a hand.

* **The Guard:** "Oi, mate. Hold up. You can't walk around with those props unsecured. Health and safety regulations. Let's see 'em."
* **Interaction:** He doesn't wait for permission. He pulls out bright orange plastic zip-ties and aggressively secures their swords into their scabbards, or ties ribbons around wands and holy symbols.
* **Mechanic:** Drawing a weapon now requires a **DC 10 Strength check** to snap the tie.
* **Failure Consequence (Fail Forward):** If they fail the check, they still snap the tie through brute force, but it looks incredibly aggressive. The Guard sighs and slaps a bright neon **"PROP VIOLATION"** wristband on them before waving them through.
  * ***Effect:*** The Rivals will notice this. Later, during the Tournament, a Rival can point out the wristband to a judge ("Disqualification! He's unsafe!"), imposing **Disadvantage** on one crucial check.

### Encounter B: The Lore Expert
A teenager wearing a t-shirt that says "The DM is Always Right" and cat ears approaches the most exotic-looking party member (Tiefling, Dragonborn, or Aasimar).

* **The Fan:** "Whoa. That is insane. Is that spirit gum or liquid latex? The texture on the horns is flawless. Did you 3D print the core?" He reaches out to poke a horn, tail, or scale without asking.
* **Mechanic:** The character must make a **DC 14 Dexterity save** to dodge the invasive poke, or a **Charisma (Intimidation)** check to growl, "Do. Not. Touch."
* **Success:** The kid's eyes go wide. "Whoa... so in character. Awesome." He snaps a selfie with them and runs off, instantly posting it to Instagram.

### Encounter C: The Alchemist
The party finds a vendor stall with a banner reading "Potions & Elixirs." It is selling "Mana Potions" (blue raspberry slushies) and "Stamina Potions" (overpriced energy drinks).

* **Interaction:** The vendor looks bored. When the party offers gold pieces, he scoffs. "Is this a prop coin? Plastic? Look, mate, I can't take props. Card or Apple Pay only. Or do you have a trade pass?"
* **Mechanic:** The Rogue must use **Sleight of Hand** to swipe a drink, or the Paladin must barter a small trinket (a 'prop' dagger or ring) to convince the vendor it's a "rare collectible."

{{descriptive
##### Item: Stamina Potion
When consumed, the sugar and caffeine rush grants the effects of *Haste* for 1 minute. Immediately after, the character suffers 1 level of Exhaustion (the crash).
}}

## Part 4: The Side Quests (Ticking Clock)
*Objective: Win items to cheat in the main tournament.*

{{note
##### The Clock
The PA system booms overhead, echoing through the hall: *"Attention cosplayers! The Tri-Fandom Qualifiers begin in 30 minutes! Please report to the staging area!"*
:
##### Constraint
The party realizes they only have time to attempt **TWO** of the three side quests before the tournament begins. They must discuss and choose which rewards will serve them best.
}}

### 1. The VR Horror Booth
* **Setting:** A sleek black kiosk surrounded by a small crowd watching a monitor. On screen, a glitchy, low-poly ghost screams. People wearing headsets are flailing wildly.
* **Game:** "Survive the Glitch." The character dons the headset and is transported to a terrifyingly realistic (to them) digital dungeon.
* **Check:** **DC 13 Wisdom saving throw** to realize the ghosts aren't real and not scream at the jump scare.
* **Reward — The Tactical Visor** *(cheap red plastic sunglasses).* These glasses filter out visual noise. The wearer ignores visual penalties (smoke, strobe lights, fog) in **Zone 1**.

### 2. The Impossible Claw Machine
* **Setting:** A glass cabinet filled with adorable, round anime-style plushies. The metal claw hanging above them looks tragically flimsy and loose. A child is crying nearby, having lost their allowance.
* **Game:** "Beat the Rig." The machine is designed to fail.
* **Check:** **DC 15 Sleight of Hand** (using thieves' tools to pick the service lock) **OR DC 16 Athletics** (subtly shaking the machine until a prize topples into the chute).
* **Reward — Gamer Grip Gel** *(a tube of neon green sticky slime).* When applied to hands or feet, it grants **Advantage** on physical checks (climbing, gripping, dancing) in **Zone 2**.

### 3. The Debate Club
* **Setting:** A messy circle of folding tables covered in energy drink cans. A group of intense, sweaty nerds are shouting about "canon events" and "power scaling."
* **Game:** "Fan Theory Debate" (e.g., "Could a level 20 Wizard defeat Goku?").
* **Check:** **DC 14 Charisma (Persuasion or Deception)**. The player must use D&D logic to construct a nonsensical but convincing argument to win over the crowd.
* **Reward — The Spoilers Cheat Sheet** *(a crumpled napkin with scribbled notes).* The napkin contains the answers to the trivia questions. Using it grants an **Auto-Success** on one round in **Zone 3**.

## Part 5: The Tri-Fandom Tournament
{{note
**Goal:** Collect 3 Tokens to qualify for the Finale.
:
**Opponents:** *Team Rules Lawyer* (Chad, Sneaky Pete, Legolad, Bathrobe Wizard). These rivals act as an arrogant foil to the party.
:
**Rival Interference Rule:** In *each* zone, Team Rules Lawyer has **1 Interference Token**. They can spend it at any time to impose **Disadvantage** on a player's check or force a re-roll. This represents them bumping elbows, flashing lights in eyes, heckling, or spilling soda.
}}

### Zone 1: The Galaxy Far, Far Away (Sci-Fi)
**Challenge.** A shooting gallery built to look like a spaceship corridor. Players must hit light-up targets with plastic Nerf blasters while dodging "return fire" (foam darts) from automated turrets and the Rivals.

***Mechanics***
* **Attack:** Make a **Ranged Weapon Attack (Dex)** vs. AC 12 targets.
* **Defense:** At the start of the round, make a **DC 14 Dexterity save** to dodge the foam hail. Failure means you are distracted and lose an action.

***Class Hooks (Agency)***
* *Monk:* Use *Deflect Missiles* to catch a foam dart and throw it back, hitting a target automatically.
* *Caster:* Use *Fog Cloud*, *Darkness*, or *Minor Illusion* to obscure the Rivals' vision, giving them **Disadvantage** on their attacks this round.

***Win Condition.*** The team must hit 5 targets before the Rivals do.

### Zone 2: The Retro Arcade (Physical)
**Challenge.** A neon-lit corner featuring two games: "Dance Dance Revolution" (rhythm) and the "High Striker" (strength hammer). The air smells of ozone and sweat.

***Mechanics***
* **Dance:** Roll **Performance** or **Acrobatics** to keep up with the arrows. The beat accelerates (DC 10 → DC 15 → DC 20).
* **Hammer:** Roll **Strength (Athletics)** vs. DC 18 to ring the bell at the top.

***Class Hooks (Agency)***
* *Barbarian/Fighter:* Spending a use of *Rage* or *Action Surge* lets the character smash the hammer with divine force, granting **Advantage** or an automatic success.
* *Bard:* *Bardic Inspiration* can be added to the Dance score, representing the Bard shouting out the rhythm.

***Win Condition.*** 3 successful checks total across the team.

### Zone 3: The Live Stage (Lore)
**Challenge.** The party sits at a panel table under bright lights. The Host asks impossible trivia questions about D&D rules or Chult history. The audience watches expectantly.

***Mechanics.*** **Contested Checks** vs. the Rivals. Players may use **Persuasion** (charming the crowd), **Deception** (making up a convincing lie), **Intimidation** (scaring the host into accepting the answer), or **Performance**.

***Class Hooks (Agency)***
* *Cleric/Paladin:* Casting *Zone of Truth* (subtly) forces a Rival to stutter and admit they don't actually know the answer, granting the party an Auto-Win for that round.
* *Warlock/Sorcerer:* Casting *Minor Illusion* creates a visual hologram of the answer, dazzling the audience and granting **Advantage**.

***Win Condition.*** Win 3 rounds of applause from the audience.

## Part 6: The Grand Finale
{{note
**Location:** The Main Stage. A massive platform with pyrotechnics, a giant screen displaying the party's faces, and a throne for the Head Judge.
:
**The Judge:** **Lord Vader** (a man in a high-quality movie replica costume).
:
**The Opponent:** **Team Rules Lawyer** (the full group assembled).
}}

### Encounter: The Battle for Hype
* **Initiative:** Roll as normal.
* **HP:** Instead of health, each team has a **"Hype Meter"** of **75 HP** (reduced from 100 to ensure the encounter doesn't drag).

***Attacks***
* **Standard Hit:** A successful attack deals **1d10 Hype damage**. This represents the crowd cheering for a solid hit.
* **Style Bonus:** If a player describes a cool move ("I backflip off the speaker!" or "I use *Thaumaturgy* to make my eyes glow red!"), add **+5 Hype damage**.
* **Magic:** Spells that would normally deal damage instead force a **Charisma save**. On a fail, the Rivals are "Stunned by the Special Effects" and lose their next turn.

{{note
##### Lair Actions (Vader)
On initiative count 20 (losing ties), Vader intervenes. Roll or choose one:
:
1. ***Force Choke.*** Vader points at a player. DC 13 Wisdom save or be Restrained by stagehands.
2. ***High Ground.*** Vader raises a platform. Attacks made from the high ground have Advantage.
3. ***Heavy Metal Music.*** Vader changes the track. All Hype damage is doubled for 1 round.
}}

**Victory.** When the Rivals' Hype hits 0, they "die" dramatically—falling over, clutching their chests, and overacting. Vader stands, slow claps, and awards the party the **Amulet**.

### The Imperial Gifts
Before the party fades away, Vader stops them. "Impressive," he breathes. "Most impressive. Take these. The Order's inventory." He tosses three plastic items that retain a spark of magic.

{{descriptive
##### Youngling Slayer 9000
A plastic extendable lightsaber. In Chult, it functions as a **+1 shortsword** that sheds dim red light.
:
##### Vocal Processor
A black voice-modulator box. In Chult, it grants **Advantage on Intimidation** checks (makes the user sound like a cyborg).
:
##### Dark Side Cookies
A tin of black-frosted cookies. Acts as a ***Potion of Greater Healing***, but the user is compelled to end their next sentence with "...as I foresaw."
}}

## Part 7: Epilogue (Return to Omu)
The party gathers around the Amulet and snaps the shards together.

{{descriptive
"The world fractures. The roar of the convention hall is silenced instantly, replaced by the heavy, oppressive silence of the underground. The cool air is slammed away by a wall of humidity and the smell of ancient rot.
:
You look down. The foam sword in your hand suddenly drags your arm down with the weight of cold, heavy steel. Your plastic armor bites into your shoulders, solid and real. The magic returns to your blood—hot, dangerous, and lethal.
:
You stand once again in the Fane of the Night Serpent. The 'fun' is over."
}}

**Ras Nsi** sits on his throne, looking exactly as you left him, though perhaps even more tired. He is shocked to see you return, but he holds to his word.

* He hands over the **Puzzle Cubes**.
* He reveals **Acererak** is the true enemy and points the way to the **Tomb of the Nine Gods**.
